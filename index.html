FSAE Rear Beam Wing
The Problem

CPP's FSAE vehicle needed more rear downforce without upsetting the aero balance. The question was whether a rear beam wing could deliver meaningful downforce gain within the constrained package size, or whether wake interference would cancel the benefit.

My Approach

Selected NACA airfoil geometries and built a full-car CFD model in ANSYS Fluent to evaluate downforce, drag, and wake behavior across 21+ simulation iterations. Results were documented in an 18-page technical report and presented to the full team for a go/no-go decision.

At a Glance
  • ~5 lb downforce increase from wing + undertray improvements
  • 21+ CFD simulations in ANSYS Fluent and SolidWorks
  • Wake interference identified as primary limiting factor
  • 18-page technical report; presented to 50+ teammates
  • Concluded beam wing incoherent with current aero package goals
Results
~5 lb
Downforce gain

18 pg
Technical report
50+

Stack
ANSYS Fluent SolidWorks CFD NACA Airfoil
What I Learned
Wake interference is a bigger constraint than wing geometry at FSAE scale. A correct answer isn't always "build it" — the most valuable outcome was a well-supported decision not to proceed.

CNC Router
The Problem

Access to a CNC router for hobby and coursework use is expensive. The goal was to build a functional 2-axis machine from scratch capable of drilling and light routing, under a $200 total budget.

My Approach

Belt-driven NEMA 17 steppers on both axes guided by V-wheel linear rails. Arduino firmware parses G-code and drives the motors with configurable acceleration profiles. A tool changer mechanism allows drill bit swaps without manual intervention.

At a Glance
  • Arduino-coded 2-axis CNC router on a 3.4×7.8 in bed
  • Belt-driven NEMA 17 steppers + V-wheel linear motion
  • Automated drill bit tool changer
  • Full build under $200 budget

Building a CNC machine teaches you where mechanical and software precision intersect. Belt backlash and step resolution decide your accuracy ceiling before firmware even matters.

Winch Sled
—
The Problem

Pull four 500g masses a height of 5ft and distance of 30m in minimum time using a 4V DC battery source, under a strict $25 budget. Speed and budget are in direct tension — higher performance motors cost more.

My Approach

Designed a 2:1 mechanical advantage pulley system to double effective force output without upgrading the motor. A flywheel stores rotational energy to sustain speed across the full 30m run. Monorail carriage built from 2×4 pine for rigidity and low friction.

At a Glance
  • Pulled 4×500g masses 30m in 1.43 seconds
  • $23 under the $25 budget
  • 2:1 pulley mechanical advantage system
  • Flywheel energy storage for consistent pull speed
  • Monorail carriage on 2×4 pine rail

What I Learned
Mechanical advantage is a cheap way to solve a power problem. Understanding the speed-force trade-off let us get a better result from a cheaper motor than trying to brute-force it with higher voltage.

Knife Design & Fabrication
The Problem

Design and fabricate a fixed-blade knife with verifiable mechanical properties — hardness and bending load — while producing a finished product with real cutting performance. Everything from CAD to heat treatment to sharpening had to be done in-house.

My Approach

A2 tool steel with a high saber grind profile was modeled in SolidWorks. Full fabrication cycle: stock removal, profiling, heat treat, temper, wet-stone sharpening. Custom PLA Wa-style handle and sheath 3D printed from the SolidWorks model. Mechanical properties verified on a Universal Instron.

At a Glance
  • A2 tool steel, high saber grind, 3D printed PLA handle
  • Heat treated and tempered to 51.5 HRC
  • 13.65 kN maximum bending load (Instron verified)
  • Fastest slicing time in class — 0.79 seconds

What I Learned
Hardness and toughness trade off against each other in tool steel — the temper cycle determines where you land. Getting to 51.5 HRC without making the blade brittle required more iteration than expected.

HVAC Cost Modeling
The Problem

How much does insulation quality actually affect the annual cost of running residential AC in California? The goal was to model that relationship using real experimental data rather than manufacturer estimates.

My Approach

Collected 1000+ data points of lux, temperature, and insulation values from a scaled box model using Vernier probing. NOAA historical temperature data for California was integrated into a MATLAB thermal model to predict annual AC cost across different insulation configurations.

At a Glance
  • 1000+ experimental data points collected via Vernier
  • MATLAB model integrating NOAA California temperature data
  • Compared multiple insulation configurations
  • Results presented as a research poster

What I Learned
Data collection is only as useful as the filtering that comes after it. Outlier removal and noise reduction took as long as building the model — which is typical of real experimental work.

Candy Crushing Device
The Problem

Design a purely mechanical device to grind tablet candies within a defined distance constraint. The deliverable was a complete engineering drawing package — not just a working concept, but a documented design ready for fabrication.

My Approach

A cam-driven crushing mechanism was designed in Fusion 360 to convert rotational input into linear crushing force within the distance constraint. Full 3D assembly, exploded views, and GD&T-compliant drawings were produced as the final deliverable.

At a Glance
  • Cam-driven mechanical crushing mechanism
  • Full 3D CAD assembly in Fusion 360
  • Exploded views and dimensioned drawings produced
